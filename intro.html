(...) 60% of Nairobi’s residents do not have access to a reliable water supply. To solve this issue, the Upper Tana-Nairobi Water Fund undertakes upstream water and soil conservation measures, resulting in improved water quality and supply.

The goal is to optimize which interventions to undertake in the Upper Tana basin , in order to improve water quality and quantity supplying Nairobi. Three ecosystem services (ES) objectives are being acted upon:

  • maximize annual water yield (AWY in 106 m3/year)
  • minimize sediment reaching streams (SDE in 106 tons of sediments eroded annually)
  • minimize soil loss for agricultural productivity (SDL) in 106 tons lost to stream annually)
About 800 scenarios were generated, arising from parametric uncertainty (4 parameters with 2 to 4 possible values each) and 15 combinations of objectives weights.
